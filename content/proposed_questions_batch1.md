### pt_0074 — hard · both

**Prompt.** In the 1920s, Soviet nationality policy known as 'korenizatsiya' ('indigenization') promoted which approach in non-Russian republics like Ukraine?

- ✓ Expanding local-language education, publishing, and administration and recruiting national cadres into party and state posts
- · Forced population transfers designed to make each republic ethnically Russian
    - *why wrong:* That approach is associated with later Stalinist deportations, not the 1920s indigenization program.
- · Abolishing the federal structure of the USSR in favor of a single unitary state
    - *why wrong:* The 1920s policy worked through, not against, the federal republic structure; the USSR was formally constituted as a federation in 1922.
- · Prohibiting any use of languages other than Russian in official life
    - *why wrong:* Russification of that scope came later, especially from the 1930s onward.

**Explanation.** Korenizatsiya was the Bolsheviks' early bid to win over non-Russian populations by supporting national languages, schools, and elites within republic boundaries. It helped consolidate republic-level identities — notably in Ukraine — before being reversed and, in the 1930s, replaced by repression of national cultural elites.
